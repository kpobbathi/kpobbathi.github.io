Science Fairs

Exploring science and engineering through hands-on projects

Overview

I have participated in science fairs in 3rd grade and 5th grade. A science fair is an event hosted by a school that offers students the opportunity to experience the practices of science and engineering for themselves. Different people or teams compete to see whose project is better and to expand their knowledge in science.

Alta Vista Elementary Science Fair 2025

An interview with everyone who participated in the science fair. 16:00 — my sister's presentation. 17:30 — my presentation.

I was on a trip in Costa Rica when my mom told me I was invited to be in Alta Vista's first ever science fair. I was pretty excited. My mom told me I had to fill in a form about my science interests so they could decide what kind of mentor I would get. I filled in physics, coding, robotics, and AI.

I spent some time thinking about what my project should be. I had a Mindstorms LEGO robotics kit, so I was going to use that. I first thought about a robot that could navigate a course and obstacles. I had a small LEGO gun, so I decided to make a robot that automatically aims at a target as long as the robot is in range.

After we got back from the trip, I started working on building my robot. I had to adjust some things, because things didn't always go the way I planned. Eventually, I managed to create a robot. As you can see, it has:

  • 2 motors to adjust the angle of the projectile
  • 1 motor to shoot it
  • A distance sensor to measure the range
  • A hub in the back with all the electronics, programming, and connections
